# Tasmanian Devil (Sarcophilus harrisii)

## Conservation Status
Endangered

## Description
The Tasmanian devil is the world's largest surviving carnivorous marsupial,
weighing 6 to 12 kg. It has a stocky build, black fur, and an extremely
powerful bite relative to body size.

## Habitat
Found only in Tasmania across a range of habitats including coastal scrub,
dry and wet eucalyptus forests, and agricultural margins. Dens in hollow
logs, caves, and dense vegetation.

## Key Threats
- Devil Facial Tumour Disease (DFTD) — a transmissible cancer that has
  reduced wild populations by over 80%
- Vehicle strikes
- Habitat fragmentation
- Competition with feral cats for food resources

## Monitoring Notes
Record individual identification (ear notches, scars) and check for facial
tumours (lumps around mouth, face, or neck). Note feeding behaviour and
den site locations. Report any diseased individuals to the Save the
Tasmanian Devil Program.
