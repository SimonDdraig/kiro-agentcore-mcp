# Platypus (Ornithorhynchus anatinus)

## Conservation Status
Near Threatened

## Description
The platypus is a semi-aquatic, egg-laying mammal endemic to eastern Australia and Tasmania.
It is one of only five extant species of monotremes.

## Habitat
Freshwater rivers, streams, and lakes in eastern Australia. Platypuses build burrows
in riverbanks and are most active during dawn and dusk.

## Key Threats
- Habitat loss from land clearing and dam construction
- Climate change reducing water flows
- Predation by foxes and dogs
- Entanglement in fishing line and yabby traps

## Monitoring Notes
Platypus sightings should include water body name, estimated size, and time of day.
Dawn and dusk surveys are most effective.
