# Frilled-neck Lizard (Chlamydosaurus kingii)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern

## Description
The frilled-neck lizard is a large arboreal agamid lizard known for its
distinctive frill that fans out when threatened. Adults reach 70 to 90 cm
in total length including the tail.

## Habitat
Tropical and warm-temperate woodlands and savanna across northern Australia
from the Kimberley to eastern Queensland. Spends most of its time in trees
and descends to the ground to feed and move between trees.

## Key Threats
- Habitat loss from pastoral clearing and mining
- Predation by feral cats
- Altered fire regimes reducing ground cover invertebrates
- Climate change affecting breeding season timing

## Monitoring Notes
Record tree species used for perching, frill display behaviour, and estimated
body size. Note presence during wet season when they are most active on the
ground. Report any predation events by feral animals.
