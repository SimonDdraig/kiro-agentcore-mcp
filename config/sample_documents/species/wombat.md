# Common Wombat (Vombatus ursinus)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern (Northern Hairy-nosed Wombat is Critically Endangered)

## Description
The common wombat is a sturdy, burrowing marsupial weighing 20 to 35 kg.
It has a broad head, short legs, and powerful claws adapted for digging
extensive burrow systems.

## Habitat
Forested and mountainous areas of south-eastern Australia including Tasmania.
Prefers slopes with well-drained soils suitable for burrowing, near grasslands
for grazing.

## Key Threats
- Sarcoptic mange (a significant and growing threat)
- Vehicle strikes (particularly at dawn and dusk)
- Habitat loss from land clearing
- Competition with rabbits for burrow sites

## Monitoring Notes
Record burrow activity (fresh diggings, droppings near entrance). Check for
signs of mange (hair loss, crusty skin). Note any road-killed individuals
and report GPS coordinates for wildlife crossing assessments.
