# Red Kangaroo (Osphranter rufus)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern

## Description
The red kangaroo is the largest living marsupial and the largest land mammal native
to Australia. Males can stand over 1.8 metres tall and weigh up to 90 kg.

## Habitat
Arid and semi-arid inland regions across most of mainland Australia. Prefers open
grasslands, scrublands, and desert plains with scattered shade trees.

## Key Threats
- Drought and water scarcity
- Vehicle collisions on outback highways
- Habitat fragmentation from pastoral land use
- Commercial and non-commercial culling

## Monitoring Notes
Record mob size, sex ratio, and presence of joeys in pouch. Note body condition
and proximity to water sources. Track grazing patterns relative to pastoral boundaries.
