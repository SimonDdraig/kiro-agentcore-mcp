# Short-beaked Echidna (Tachyglossus aculeatus)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern

## Description
The short-beaked echidna is one of only two egg-laying mammals (monotremes)
in Australia, alongside the platypus. It is covered in coarse hair and spines,
weighs 2 to 7 kg, and has a distinctive elongated snout.

## Habitat
Found across all of mainland Australia and Tasmania in a wide range of habitats
from alpine meadows to arid deserts, forests, and suburban gardens. Highly
adaptable provided ant and termite food sources are available.

## Key Threats
- Vehicle strikes (a leading cause of mortality)
- Habitat loss and fragmentation
- Predation by dogs and foxes
- Bushfires destroying foraging habitat

## Monitoring Notes
Record activity type (foraging, mating train, burrowing). Note soil type
and termite mound proximity. During breeding season (June to August), watch
for mating trains of up to ten males following a single female.
