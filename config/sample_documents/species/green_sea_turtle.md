# Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas)

## Conservation Status
Vulnerable (listed as Endangered under EPBC Act for some populations)

## Description
The green sea turtle is one of the largest sea turtles, with adults weighing
up to 200 kg and a carapace length of around 1 metre. Named for the green
colour of its body fat, not its shell.

## Habitat
Tropical and subtropical waters around the Australian coast, particularly
the Great Barrier Reef, Torres Strait, and north-western Australia. Nests
on sandy beaches and forages on seagrass beds and algae-covered reefs.

## Key Threats
- Marine debris and plastic ingestion
- Boat strikes
- Loss of nesting beaches from coastal development and sea level rise
- Bycatch in fishing nets and trawlers
- Predation of eggs by foxes and feral pigs

## Monitoring Notes
Record carapace length, tag numbers (flipper and PIT tags), and nesting
activity. During nesting season (October to March), count nest sites and
record hatching success rates. Report any stranded or injured turtles to
marine wildlife rescue.
