# Koala (Phascolarctos cinereus)

## Conservation Status
Vulnerable (listed as Endangered in NSW, QLD, and ACT)

## Description
The koala is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. It is the only
extant representative of the family Phascolarctidae.

## Habitat
Eucalyptus woodlands and forests along the eastern and south-eastern coast of Australia.
Koalas are highly dependent on specific eucalyptus species for food and shelter.

## Key Threats
- Habitat loss from urbanisation and land clearing
- Bushfires (particularly the 2019-2020 season)
- Disease (chlamydia)
- Vehicle strikes and dog attacks
- Climate change reducing eucalyptus nutritional quality

## Monitoring Notes
Record tree species being used, presence of joeys, and any signs of disease
(discharge from eyes or nose). Note proximity to roads and urban areas.
