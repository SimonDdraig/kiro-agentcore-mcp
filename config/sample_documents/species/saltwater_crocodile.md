# Saltwater Crocodile (Crocodylus porosus)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern (protected in all Australian states and territories)

## Description
The saltwater crocodile is the largest living reptile, with males reaching over
6 metres in length. It is an apex predator found in northern Australia.

## Habitat
Coastal and estuarine environments including river mouths, mangrove swamps,
tidal flats, and freshwater billabongs across northern Australia from Broome
to Gladstone.

## Key Threats
- Illegal poaching
- Habitat degradation from coastal development
- Nest predation by feral pigs
- Human-wildlife conflict in populated areas

## Monitoring Notes
Record estimated body length, location (GPS), and behaviour. Note nesting sites
during breeding season (November to March). Report any human-crocodile interactions
to local wildlife authorities immediately.
