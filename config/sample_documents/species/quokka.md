# Quokka (Setonix brachyurus)

## Conservation Status
Vulnerable

## Description
The quokka is a small wallaby weighing 2.5 to 5 kg, known for its friendly
appearance and seemingly smiling expression. It is primarily nocturnal and
herbivorous.

## Habitat
Restricted to Rottnest Island, Bald Island, and scattered mainland populations
in south-western Western Australia. Prefers dense vegetation near swamps and
watercourses on the mainland, and a variety of habitats on Rottnest Island.

## Key Threats
- Predation by foxes and cats (mainland populations)
- Habitat loss from land clearing and altered fire regimes
- Human interaction on Rottnest Island (feeding, handling)
- Climate change reducing freshwater availability

## Monitoring Notes
Record population counts, body condition, and joey presence. On Rottnest
Island, note any signs of human-caused injury or illness from inappropriate
feeding. On the mainland, record predator signs near quokka habitat.
