# Eastern Brown Snake (Pseudonaja textilis)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern

## Description
The eastern brown snake is the second most venomous land snake in the world.
It is slender and fast-moving, typically 1.1 to 1.8 metres in length, with
colour ranging from pale brown to almost black.

## Habitat
Found across eastern Australia from Cape York to South Australia. Inhabits
dry woodlands, grasslands, farmland, and suburban fringes. Often found near
human habitation where mice and rats are abundant.

## Key Threats
- Habitat loss from agriculture and urbanisation
- Road mortality
- Persecution by humans due to fear
- Cane toad ingestion (secondary poisoning)

## Monitoring Notes
Record length estimate, colour morph, and behaviour. Note proximity to
dwellings and livestock. Avoid handling — observe from a safe distance.
Report any defensive displays or bite incidents.
