# Lace Monitor / Goanna (Varanus varius)

## Conservation Status
Least Concern

## Description
The lace monitor is Australia's second-largest lizard, reaching up to 2 metres
in total length. It is a powerful climber with strong claws, a long forked
tongue, and distinctive dark grey-blue banding with cream or yellow spots.

## Habitat
Eastern Australian forests and woodlands from Cape York to south-eastern
South Australia. Arboreal and terrestrial, commonly seen climbing trees or
basking on fallen logs and rock outcrops.

## Key Threats
- Cane toad ingestion (lethal secondary poisoning in northern populations)
- Road mortality
- Habitat clearing reducing tree hollows used for nesting
- Persecution near poultry farms

## Monitoring Notes
Record body length estimate, tree species used, and foraging behaviour.
Note any cane toad presence in the area. During nesting season (November
to December), check termite mounds for egg-laying activity.
