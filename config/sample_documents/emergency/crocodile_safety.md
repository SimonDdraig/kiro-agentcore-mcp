# Crocodile Safety Procedures

## Emergency Contact
- Emergency services: **000**
- Local wildlife authority crocodile hotline (NT): 1800 019 090

## Risk Areas
Saltwater crocodiles inhabit all waterways, estuaries, and coastal areas
in northern Australia from Broome (WA) to Gladstone (QLD). Freshwater
crocodiles are found further inland. Assume every body of water in
crocodile country contains crocodiles.

## Prevention
- Obey all crocodile warning signs — they are placed based on confirmed sightings
- Stay at least 5 metres from the water's edge at all times
- Never clean fish, prepare food, or wash dishes at the water's edge
- Camp at least 50 metres from the water and 2 metres above the high-water mark
- Never swim in water that has not been declared safe by local authorities
- Be extra cautious at dawn, dusk, and night when crocodiles are most active
- Keep children and pets well away from the water's edge

## If a Crocodile Attack Occurs
1. Call 000 immediately
2. Fight back — target the eyes, nostrils, and palatal valve (flap at back of throat)
3. If grabbed, do NOT roll with the crocodile — resist the death roll
4. Apply pressure to wounds and control bleeding
5. Evacuate the patient to medical care as quickly as possible
6. Crocodile bite wounds carry high infection risk — antibiotics are essential

## Ranger Notes
Report all crocodile sightings with GPS coordinates, estimated size, and
behaviour. Install and maintain warning signage at known habitats. During
flood events, crocodiles may appear in unexpected locations — increase
patrols and public warnings accordingly.
