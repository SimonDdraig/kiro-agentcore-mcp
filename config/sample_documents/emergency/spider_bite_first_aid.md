# Spider Bite First Aid Procedures

## Emergency Contact
- Emergency services: **000**
- Poisons Information Centre: **13 11 26**

## Funnel-web Spider Bite
Funnel-web spiders (Atrax and Hadronyche species) are found in eastern
Australia. Their bite is a medical emergency.

### First Aid
1. Call 000 immediately
2. Apply the Pressure Immobilisation Technique (same as snakebite):
   - Firm broad bandage over the bite site
   - Wrap the entire limb towards the heart
   - Splint the limb to prevent movement
3. Keep the patient calm and still
4. Monitor breathing and be prepared to perform CPR
5. Antivenom is available at all major hospitals

## Redback Spider Bite
Redback spiders are found across all of Australia. Bites are painful but
rarely life-threatening with access to medical care.

### First Aid
1. Apply a cold pack (NOT ice directly on skin) to reduce pain
2. Do NOT apply a pressure bandage — this can worsen pain
3. Seek medical attention — antivenom is available if symptoms are severe
4. Monitor for symptoms: increasing pain, sweating, nausea, abdominal pain

## Other Spiders
Most other Australian spiders are not considered dangerous. Clean the bite
with antiseptic and apply a cold pack. Seek medical attention if symptoms
develop or the patient is a child.

## Ranger Notes
Shake out boots, gloves, and clothing before putting them on in the field.
Check under toilet seats, log piles, and rock ledges. Funnel-web spiders
are most active after rain and during warm humid nights.
