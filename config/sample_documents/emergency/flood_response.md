# Flood Emergency Response Procedures

## Emergency Contact
- Emergency services: **000**
- SES (State Emergency Service): **132 500**

## Flood Warning Levels
1. **Minor**: Low-lying areas near rivers may be affected. Monitor Bureau of Meteorology alerts.
2. **Moderate**: Inundation of low-lying areas expected. Evacuate campsites near waterways.
3. **Major**: Extensive flooding. Close all park access roads. Full evacuation of visitors and staff.

## Ranger Response Protocol
1. Monitor Bureau of Meteorology river height gauges and radar
2. Close river crossings and low-lying tracks at first sign of rising water
3. Evacuate visitors from campgrounds within 500 metres of waterways
4. Barricade flooded roads and install warning signage
5. Report to incident controller at park headquarters
6. Never attempt to drive, walk, or swim through floodwater

## Key Rules
- If it's flooded, forget it — no exceptions
- Flash flooding can occur with little warning in gorges and creek beds
- Crocodile displacement is common during floods in northern regions

## Post-Flood Assessment
- Inspect all bridges, causeways, and river crossings for structural damage
- Check walking tracks for washouts and landslips
- Record debris lines and high-water marks with GPS
- Assess impact on wildlife nesting sites (turtles, platypus, waterbirds)
- Test water quality before reopening swimming areas
