# Heat Exposure and Survival in Harsh Climates

## Emergency Contact
- Emergency services: **000**

## Heat-Related Illness Stages

### Heat Exhaustion
- Symptoms: heavy sweating, weakness, cold/clammy skin, nausea, dizziness, fast weak pulse
- Action: move to shade, lie down, sip water, apply cool wet cloths, loosen clothing

### Heat Stroke (MEDICAL EMERGENCY)
- Symptoms: body temperature above 40°C, hot dry skin (no sweating), rapid strong pulse, confusion, loss of consciousness
- Action: call 000 immediately, move to shade, cool rapidly with water, fan the patient, do NOT give fluids if unconscious

## Prevention for Rangers
- Carry a minimum of 1 litre of water per hour of patrol in temperatures above 35°C
- Wear broad-brimmed hat, long sleeves, and sunscreen (SPF 50+)
- Schedule patrols for early morning or late afternoon in summer
- Use the buddy system — never patrol alone in extreme heat
- Know the signs in yourself: if you stop sweating, seek shade immediately

## Survival in Arid Environments
- Stay with your vehicle if it breaks down — it is easier to spot than a person
- Conserve water by minimising exertion during the hottest part of the day (11am to 3pm)
- Create shade using a tarp, vehicle bonnet, or vegetation
- Signal for help using a mirror, bright clothing, or vehicle horn at regular intervals
- If you must walk, travel at night or during dawn/dusk and follow drainage lines towards civilisation

## Cold Exposure (Hypothermia)
Alpine and southern regions can experience rapid temperature drops.

- Symptoms: shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness, loss of coordination
- Action: shelter from wind and rain, remove wet clothing, wrap in dry layers and emergency blanket, share body warmth, give warm sweet drinks if conscious
- Call 000 if symptoms are severe or the patient stops shivering (this indicates worsening hypothermia)

## Ranger Notes
All vehicles must carry an emergency survival kit: 10 litres of water,
first aid kit, emergency blanket, signalling mirror, torch, and UHF radio.
File a patrol plan with headquarters before entering remote areas.
