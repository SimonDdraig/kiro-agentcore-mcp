# Blue Mountains National Park Management Plan

## Overview
Blue Mountains National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage-listed area in New South
Wales, covering approximately 267,000 hectares west of Sydney. The park protects
extensive eucalyptus forests, dramatic sandstone cliff lines, and deep gorges.
It is managed by NSW National Parks and Wildlife Service.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of eucalyptus forest ecosystems and endemic species
2. Bushfire management and post-fire recovery (severely impacted in 2019-2020)
3. Weed and feral animal control (foxes, cats, deer, pigs)
4. Management of high visitor numbers at key sites (Three Sisters, Wentworth Falls)
5. Protection of the Wollemi pine (Wollemia nobilis) — a living fossil

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Dec-Feb): High bushfire risk, restrict access on catastrophic fire days
- Autumn (Mar-May): Optimal bushwalking season, track maintenance period
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Snow possible at higher elevations, waterfall flows peak
- Spring (Sep-Nov): Wildflower season, increased visitor numbers

## Key Species
- Wollemi pine (Wollemia nobilis) — Critically Endangered (exact location classified)
- Greater glider (Petauroides volans) — Endangered
- Spotted-tailed quoll (Dasyurus maculatus) — Endangered
- Blue Mountains water skink (Eulamprus leuraensis) — Endangered

## Key Threats
- Bushfire (increasing frequency and intensity)
- Urban encroachment from western Sydney expansion
- Phytophthora root rot affecting native vegetation
- Climate change reducing moisture in hanging swamps
