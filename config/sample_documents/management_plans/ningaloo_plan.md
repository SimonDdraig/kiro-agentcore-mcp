# Ningaloo Coast Management Plan

## Overview
The Ningaloo Coast in Western Australia extends over 300 kilometres along the
North West Cape, encompassing Ningaloo Marine Park and Cape Range National Park.
It is a UNESCO World Heritage site recognised for its fringing reef — one of the
longest in the world — and the adjacent arid karst landscape. Managed jointly by
the WA Department of Biodiversity, Conservation and Attractions and traditional
owners.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of the fringing coral reef and whale shark aggregation
2. Marine turtle nesting beach management (loggerhead, green, hawksbill)
3. Subterranean fauna conservation in Cape Range karst caves
4. Sustainable tourism and snorkelling/diving access management
5. Feral goat and fox control in Cape Range National Park

## Seasonal Considerations
- Autumn (Mar-May): Whale shark season begins, coral spawning
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Humpback whale migration, manta ray aggregation
- Spring (Sep-Nov): Turtle nesting season begins, wildflowers in Cape Range
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Turtle hatching, extreme heat, cyclone risk

## Key Species
- Whale shark (Rhincodon typus) — Vulnerable, seasonal aggregation
- Loggerhead turtle (Caretta caretta) — Endangered, major nesting site
- Blind cave eel (Ophisternon candidum) — subterranean endemic
- Ningaloo cave shrimp (Stygiocaris lancifera) — cave-adapted endemic

## Key Threats
- Coral bleaching from marine heatwaves
- Boat anchor damage to reef
- Light pollution disrupting turtle hatchling orientation
- Feral predators on turtle nesting beaches
