# Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park Management Plan

## Overview
Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park covers 1,326 square kilometres in the southern
Northern Territory. The park is owned by the Anangu traditional owners and
jointly managed with Parks Australia. It is a dual UNESCO World Heritage site
recognised for both cultural and natural values.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of Anangu cultural sites and traditional knowledge (Tjukurpa)
2. Management of arid zone ecosystems and spinifex grasslands
3. Feral animal control (camels, foxes, cats, rabbits)
4. Fire management using traditional Anangu patch-burning practices
5. Water source protection in an extremely arid environment

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Extreme heat (45°C+), restricted climbing/walking times
- Autumn (Mar-May): Cooling temperatures, wildflower displays after rain
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Cold nights, peak visitor season, optimal walking conditions
- Spring (Sep-Nov): Warming rapidly, reptile activity increases

## Key Species
- Great desert skink (Liopholis kintorei) — Vulnerable, culturally significant
- Thorny devil (Moloch horridus) — arid specialist
- Black-footed rock-wallaby (Petrogale lateralis) — Vulnerable
- Spinifex hopping mouse (Notomys alexis)

## Key Threats
- Feral cats and foxes predating native mammals
- Feral camels damaging waterholes and vegetation
- Buffel grass invasion displacing native spinifex
- Climate change intensifying drought periods
