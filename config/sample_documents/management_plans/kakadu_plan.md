# Kakadu National Park Management Plan

## Overview
Kakadu National Park is a protected area in the Northern Territory of Australia,
covering nearly 20,000 square kilometres. It is jointly managed by Aboriginal
traditional owners and Parks Australia.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of wetland ecosystems and migratory bird habitats
2. Management of invasive species (cane toads, mimosa, para grass)
3. Fire management using traditional Aboriginal burning practices
4. Monitoring of saltwater crocodile populations
5. Protection of cultural heritage sites

## Seasonal Considerations
- Wet season (Nov-Apr): Limited access to many areas, focus on aerial surveys
- Dry season (May-Oct): Ground-based surveys, controlled burns, visitor management

## Key Species
- Saltwater crocodile (Crocodylus porosus)
- Magpie goose (Anseranas semipalmata)
- Hooded parrot (Psephotellus dissimilis)
- Northern quoll (Dasyurus hallucatus) — Endangered
