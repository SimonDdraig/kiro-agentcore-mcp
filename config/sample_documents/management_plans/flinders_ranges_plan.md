# Flinders Ranges National Park Management Plan

## Overview
Flinders Ranges National Park covers 95,000 hectares in South Australia,
approximately 430 kilometres north of Adelaide. The park protects the iconic
Wilpena Pound (Ikara), a natural amphitheatre of ancient geological significance.
It is co-managed with the Adnyamathanha traditional owners and administered by
the SA Department for Environment and Water.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Yellow-footed rock-wallaby population recovery
2. Feral predator control (foxes, cats) to protect native mammals
3. Feral goat removal to reduce grazing pressure on native vegetation
4. Protection of Ediacaran fossil sites (560 million year old fossils)
5. Adnyamathanha cultural heritage site management

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Extreme heat (40°C+), restricted walking times
- Autumn (Mar-May): Cooling temperatures, good walking conditions
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Cold nights, frost possible, clear skies for astronomy
- Spring (Sep-Nov): Wildflower season, optimal wildlife viewing

## Key Species
- Yellow-footed rock-wallaby (Petrogale xanthopus) — Vulnerable, flagship species
- Short-beaked echidna (Tachyglossus aculeatus) — common in park
- Wedge-tailed eagle (Aquila audax) — apex aerial predator
- Western grey kangaroo (Macropus fuliginosus)

## Key Threats
- Feral cats and foxes predating rock-wallaby populations
- Feral goats competing for food and degrading habitat
- Drought intensification under climate change
- Erosion of walking tracks from flash flooding
