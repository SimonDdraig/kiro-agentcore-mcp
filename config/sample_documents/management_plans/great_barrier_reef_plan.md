# Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Management Plan

## Overview
The Great Barrier Reef Marine Park stretches over 2,300 kilometres along the
Queensland coast, covering approximately 344,400 square kilometres. It is the
world's largest coral reef system and a UNESCO World Heritage site, managed by
the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority (GBRMPA).

## Conservation Priorities
1. Coral reef health monitoring and bleaching response
2. Water quality improvement from agricultural runoff reduction
3. Crown-of-thorns starfish (COTS) population control
4. Protection of marine turtle nesting sites
5. Sustainable fishing and tourism management through zoning

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Nov-Mar): Coral spawning, marine stinger season, cyclone risk
- Autumn (Apr-May): Post-cyclone damage assessment, turtle hatchling season ends
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Peak tourism, whale migration through the reef
- Spring (Sep-Oct): Turtle nesting begins, optimal reef survey conditions

## Key Species
- Green sea turtle (Chelonia mydas) — Vulnerable
- Dugong (Dugong dugon) — Vulnerable
- Humpback whale (Megaptera novaeangliae) — seasonal migrant
- Maori wrasse (Cheilinus undulatus) — Endangered
- Staghorn coral (Acropora spp.) — climate-sensitive indicator species

## Key Threats
- Coral bleaching from rising sea temperatures
- Crown-of-thorns starfish outbreaks
- Agricultural sediment and nutrient runoff
- Tropical cyclone damage
- Illegal fishing and poaching
