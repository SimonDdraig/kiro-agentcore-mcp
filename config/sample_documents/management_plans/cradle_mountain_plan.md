# Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park Management Plan

## Overview
Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park covers 161,000 hectares in the
central highlands of Tasmania. It is part of the Tasmanian Wilderness World
Heritage Area and is managed by Tasmania Parks and Wildlife Service. The park
contains ancient Gondwanan rainforest, alpine moorlands, and Australia's deepest
natural freshwater lake.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of alpine and sub-alpine ecosystems sensitive to climate change
2. Tasmanian devil population monitoring and disease management (DFTD)
3. Pencil pine (Athrotaxis cupressoides) protection from fire
4. Phytophthora root rot containment through boot-wash stations
5. Overland Track management (walker numbers, hut maintenance)

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Peak walking season, Overland Track bookings required
- Autumn (Mar-May): Fagus (deciduous beech) turning — only native deciduous tree
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Snow cover, limited access, cross-country skiing
- Spring (Sep-Nov): Snowmelt, waterfall flows peak, wildflower displays

## Key Species
- Tasmanian devil (Sarcophilus harrisii) — Endangered (DFTD)
- Platypus (Ornithorhynchus anatinus) — common in park waterways
- Pencil pine (Athrotaxis cupressoides) — ancient conifer, fire-sensitive
- Mountain shrimp (Anaspides tasmaniae) — Gondwanan relict crustacean

## Key Threats
- Devil facial tumour disease (DFTD) decimating devil populations
- Climate change reducing snow cover and alpine habitat
- Wildfire destroying fire-sensitive pencil pine stands (1,000+ year recovery)
- Phytophthora root rot spread by walkers
