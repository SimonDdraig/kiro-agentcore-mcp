# Rottnest Island Management Plan

## Overview
Rottnest Island (Wadjemup) is an A-class reserve located 18 km off the coast
of Fremantle, Western Australia. The island covers 19 square kilometres and is
managed by the Rottnest Island Authority. It is the primary stronghold for the
quokka population and holds significant cultural importance to the Whadjuk
Noongar people.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection and monitoring of the quokka (Setonix brachyurus) population
2. Coastal and marine habitat protection including seagrass meadows and coral reefs
3. Management of visitor impacts (over 750,000 visitors annually)
4. Weed control and native vegetation restoration
5. Recognition and protection of Whadjuk Noongar cultural heritage

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Peak visitor season, increased quokka-human interactions
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Breeding season for quokkas, reduced visitor numbers
- Year-round: Marine patrols for reef protection and boating compliance

## Key Species
- Quokka (Setonix brachyurus) — Vulnerable
- New Zealand fur seal (Arctocephalus forsteri)
- Osprey (Pandion haliaetus)
- Humpback whale (Megaptera novaeangliae) — seasonal migration

## Visitor Management
- No private vehicles permitted — cycling and bus transport only
- Feeding or handling quokkas is prohibited (fines apply)
- Drone use restricted to protect wildlife
- Camping limited to designated areas with booking required
