# Pilbara Region Management Plan

## Overview
The Pilbara region in north-western Western Australia covers approximately
500,000 square kilometres of ancient landscape including Karijini National Park,
Millstream-Chichester National Park, and the Burrup Peninsula. The region
contains some of the oldest rock formations on Earth and significant Aboriginal
rock art sites.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of permanent freshwater pools (gorge systems) critical for endemic species
2. Management of feral animals (cats, foxes, donkeys, camels)
3. Fire management to protect spinifex grassland mosaics
4. Monitoring of threatened species including the Pilbara olive python and ghost bat
5. Protection of Aboriginal rock art and cultural heritage sites from mining impacts

## Seasonal Considerations
- Summer (Nov-Mar): Extreme heat (45°C+), cyclone risk, restricted field operations
- Winter (May-Sep): Optimal survey period, mild temperatures, dry conditions
- Cyclone season: Monitor Bureau of Meteorology warnings, secure infrastructure

## Key Species
- Pilbara olive python (Liasis olivaceus barroni) — Vulnerable
- Ghost bat (Macroderma gigas) — Vulnerable
- Northern quoll (Dasyurus hallucatus) — Endangered
- Pilbara leaf-nosed bat (Rhinonicteris aurantia) — Vulnerable

## Key Threats
- Mining operations and associated infrastructure
- Feral herbivores degrading riparian vegetation
- Altered fire regimes from pastoral management
- Climate change increasing drought frequency
