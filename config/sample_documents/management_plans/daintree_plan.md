# Daintree Rainforest Management Plan

## Overview
The Daintree Rainforest in far north Queensland is one of the oldest surviving
tropical rainforests on Earth, estimated at over 135 million years old. The
region covers approximately 1,200 square kilometres and is part of the Wet
Tropics World Heritage Area. It is co-managed by Eastern Kuku Yalanji
traditional owners and Queensland Parks and Wildlife Service.

## Conservation Priorities
1. Protection of ancient Gondwanan plant lineages and endemic species
2. Cassowary habitat connectivity and road-strike reduction
3. Feral pig control to prevent rainforest floor disturbance
4. Riparian zone protection along the Daintree River
5. Sustainable tourism management at Mossman Gorge and Cape Tribulation

## Seasonal Considerations
- Wet season (Dec-Apr): Heavy rainfall, road closures, creek crossings impassable
- Dry season (May-Nov): Peak visitor season, fire risk on rainforest margins
- Year-round: Cassowary breeding and fruit dispersal monitoring

## Key Species
- Southern cassowary (Casuarius casuarius johnsonii) — Endangered
- Bennett's tree-kangaroo (Dendrolagus bennettianus) — Near Threatened
- Boyd's forest dragon (Lophosaurus boydii) — rainforest specialist
- Musky rat-kangaroo (Hypsiprymnodon moschatus) — world's smallest kangaroo

## Key Threats
- Cassowary road strikes on Cape Tribulation Road
- Feral pigs destroying forest floor habitat
- Cyclone damage to canopy structure
- Climate change shifting altitudinal species ranges
